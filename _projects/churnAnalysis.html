There are a few ways to combat churn. One potent option is using something called Health Snapshots. Health Snapshots essentially grade clients based on actionable parameters, ideally providing insights for proactive actions. For example, an ice-cream shop could identify that customers who purchased their mint-chocolate chip ice cream were more loyal than other customers. Making the availability of mint-chocolate chip more prominent or offering free samples might be prudent actions to take.

Beneath the surface, the shop owner is essentially categorizing or grading customers based on their purchases. If customers don't purchase mint-chocolate chip, their grade is lower. Most likely, other parameters are also influencing this process, such as cost, shop hours, and customer service.

Abundant customer data is available, and many companies already have this data at their disposal, albeit sometimes in a somewhat disorganized state. By employing statistical analysis and, of course, a bit of coffee, we can estimate a retention grade for each customer. This process will yield a prioritization of parameters. While numerous parameters will be actionable, it's important to recognize that not all will be. For certain cases, knowing each customer's grade will suffice, particularly if the number of parameters is limited. However, when dealing with a substantial number of parameters, it becomes prudent to also assign grades to these individual parameters.

I will be walking through a process of grading fictional real estate teams using a CRM program. This walkthrough will demonstrate how to establish health scores for each team and parameter. Throughout this journey, I will elucidate the theory behind each step, employing graphs and mathematical concepts for illustrative purposes.
