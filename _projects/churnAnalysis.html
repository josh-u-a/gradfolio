Combatting churn requires innovative solutions, and one powerful method is through analyzing customer Health Snapshots. Scoring clients based on these snapshots offers insights that drive proactive strategies. For instance, an ice-cream shop could discover the loyalty of mint-chocolate chip ice cream buyers, prompting actions like emphasizing its availability or offering samples.

In the context of the ice-cream shop, a customer's purchases influence their score, resulting in lower scores for those who don't buy mint-chocolate chip. Other factors like cost, shop hours, and service also contribute to these scores.

Armed with a wealth of customer data (captured in the health snapshot), we can leverage the power of statistics, and coffee, to estimate retention scores for customers. This process involves prioritizing and grading parameters for each customer. The parameter grades collectively build the retention score for each customer. These retention scores not only guide our focus but also inform the interventions we employ.

Repeating this process on a regular basis crafts a unique narrative for each customer. When other records are tracked similarly, we can glean insights into the changes or behaviors that significantly impact customer satisfaction and loyalty.

In this post, I'll delve into the process by scoring fictitious real estate teams that utilize a subscription software program. Through this exploration, I'll establish health scores for both teams and parameters, employing graphs and mathematics to enhance understanding.
